#Metoo and #Timesup: The Words of the Movements

The following text analysis shows the start of the #MeToo and #Timesup movements until the first day of 2019. #MeToo and #Times up have changed the global conversation about sexual harassment and assault. The following data shows the sentiments between these two movements and how much they affect each other.

#MeToo and #Timesup Movement

In 1997 activist Tarana Burke heard the story of a 13 year old sexual abuse survivor, Tarana told the New York Times “I didn’t have a response or a way to help her in that moment, and I couldn’t even say ‘me too,’”. She began the #MeToo in 2006.

On the 5th of October 2017 The New York Times published an article that accuses Harvey Weinstein of sexual harassment and assault that spans over three decades. Eleven days later actress Alyssa Milano encourages people to use the #MeToo to share their stories of sexual abuse and assault to show the extend of the problem. It becomes a global phenomenon over the next week.

Over the next few months different actresses and media professionals would meet to create the #Timesup movement. These two anti-sexual harassment and women's empowerment movements changed the conversation across the entire world. Spawning hundreds of versions of the hashtags in different languages across the globe.

#MeToo and #Timesup Tweets over time

With both of these movements you can see that they started with an explosive energy, becoming viral around an event. With #MeToo it was ignited by Harvey Weinstein’s allegations and how it represented sexual abuse and harassment being ignored or hidden. With #Timesup the movement became viral around the Golden Globes when women in the entertainment industry all decided to wear black to highlight issues surrounding sexual abuse and harassment.

#MeToo and #Timesup Wordclouds

In both of these wordclouds you can see how there is a large crossover in the words which appear across the two hastags.

#MeToo and #Timesup Most Popular Terms

In both movements abuse, assault and harassment are in the top four terms used.

#MeToo and #Timesup Most Popular Pair of Term

#MeToo and #Timesup Sentiments

From the sentiments you can see that though negative emotion is more prevalent the movement has strong tones of empowerment and support for women and sexual abuse survivors. This is present as both movements are were created to give a voice to those who did not feel like they had one.

#MeToo and #Timesup Positive and Negitive Tones

Both movements overall have more negative sentiments then positive, but both hashtags, particularly #MeToo is used to share stories of sexual abuse and harassment so the large proportion of positive tweets shows that the hastags are also used to empower and support survivors of sexual abuse and harassment.

The #MeToo and #Timesup movements have changed the conversation around sexual assault and harassment. Since they started in October 2017 and January 2018, respectively, they have each received millions of tweets in their own hashtags and in the variations that have spawned from them. This analysis shows that even though the movements are used as a platform to share negative experiences there is still a positive undertones.
